# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Loyalty Radar should be a practical, respectful place to improve open-source loyalty intelligence. Contributors, maintainers, and community members are expected to:

- discuss ideas and evidence without attacking people;
- give specific, actionable technical feedback;
- respect different languages, regions, experience levels, and travel patterns;
- disclose uncertainty and source limitations;
- protect personal data and avoid posting private account information;
- follow forum, publisher, program, and community rules;
- avoid instructions that enable fraud, access-control evasion, harassment, or abuse.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discriminatory language, threats, doxxing, sexualized conduct, sustained disruption, impersonation, and publishing another person's private information without permission.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, Discussions, release channels, community events, and public spaces where someone is representing Loyalty Radar.

## Reporting and enforcement

For conduct concerns, use the repository's private security-reporting channel and state that the report concerns community conduct. Do not include sensitive details in a public issue. If that channel is not available, open a minimal public issue asking the maintainer to establish private contact.

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions, restrict participation, temporarily suspend access, or permanently ban participants when behavior threatens the project or community. Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented privately, and avoid unnecessary disclosure of the people involved.

Retaliation against someone who reports a concern in good faith is not permitted.
